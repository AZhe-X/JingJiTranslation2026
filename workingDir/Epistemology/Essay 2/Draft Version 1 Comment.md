### Consideration (Not a Change Request)

The essay's central insight is what I call the **"digestion problem"**: phenomena must be converted into belief through cognitive processing before serving as justification. Bob 3 (emotional reverie, no belief formed) is the load-bearing example. 

The current TN doesn't mention this, and that's probably correct — a TN shouldn't summarize the argument. But a reader's introduction or essay headnote (if the collection includes such apparatus) might note: "This essay argues that the truth condition is methodologically impossible and that even phenomenal justification reduces to belief through what the author calls 'digestion.'"

This is a suggestion for the broader editorial apparatus, not a change to the current TN.
