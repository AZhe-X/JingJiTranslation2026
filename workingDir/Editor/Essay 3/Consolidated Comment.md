## Summary of Strengths (Unanimous)

### 1. Experiential Sequence — Intact and Load-Bearing
The four-stage progression is the essay's argument:
1. **Sisyphus** — mythic, requires heroic revolt
2. **Office worker** — mundane, alienated
3. **Entrepreneur** — self-directed, still "transcendent"
4. **Cat owner** — immanent, absurdity never arrives

All advisors confirm this sequence is preserved. The reader is led through progressively simpler cases until absurdity dissolves entirely. This IS the philosophical work — and it's intact.

### 2. "Immanent Meaning" (内生的意义) — Excellent Choice
Unanimous endorsement. "Immanent" captures:
- Generated from within (not received from outside)
- Non-transcendent (not pointing beyond itself)
- Self-sufficient (not requiring articulation or justification)

Multiple advisors note this is philosophically precise and avoids over-technicalizing.

### 3. Cat Example — Full Philosophical Force
The cat example delivers the punch: absurdity *never arrives*. All advisors confirm this is preserved:
> "simply because we want to raise a cat, we like living with a cat, and that is all"

Buddhism calls this "koan-punchline" — the problem was never real. Phenomenology notes meaning is "lived, not sought." Existentialism notes this is "philosophically bold" — it suggests Camus's question was malformed.
