## Overall Assessment: GOOD

The translation preserves the essay's philosophical architecture and its function as a graduated thought-experiment that dissolves the absurdity problematic rather than resolving it.

## Philosophical Strengths

### 1. The Graduated Sequence Is Intact
The essay moves through four levels:
- Sisyphus (mythic, heroic revolt)
- Office worker (mundane, alienated)
- Entrepreneur (self-directed, still transcendent)
- Cat owner (immanent, absurdity-immune)

Each level strips away another layer of "transcendence" until we reach a case where the absurdity question *cannot even arise*. The translation preserves this sequence perfectly.

### 2. "Immanent Meaning" (内生的意义)
Excellent rendering. "Immanent" captures:
- Generated from within (not received from outside)
- Non-transcendent (not pointing beyond itself)
- Self-sufficient (not requiring justification)

This is precisely the post-metaphysical stance: meaning that doesn't need to appeal to anything beyond phenomena.

### 3. The Alienation Diagnosis
"Alienation" for 异化 is correct and carries the right Marxist resonance. The author's move is to reframe Camus's "absurdity" as a misdescription of alienation — the dissonance comes from externalized meaning, not from cosmic silence.

### 4. The Dissolution vs. Resolution Point
TN2 makes this explicit: "This dissolves the Camusian problematic rather than resolving it." This is the crucial metaphysical claim. Sisyphus and Camus operate within a framework where meaning must be *recovered* after loss; the cat owner operates in a framework where meaning was never at risk.

### 5. Title Translation
"Work" for 班 is perfect — colloquial, mundane, non-heroic. "Labor" or "toil" would import dignity the author strips away. The title's juxtaposition (myth/mundane/domestic) is preserved.

## One Observation

The phrase "transcendent external existences" (超越的外在存在) could read awkwardly to readers unfamiliar with the technical sense. But context makes it clear — gods and universe as what Sisyphus rebels against. No change needed.

## Connection to the Framework

This essay anticipates the 诗意 essay's distinction:
- Seeking meaning externally = 沉醉的梦境 (intoxicated, pre-critical)
- Revolt against absurdity = transition moment
- Immanent meaning = 清醒的梦境 (lucid, post-metaphysical)

The cat owner already lives poetically without needing the vocabulary for it. The essay performs what the later essays theorize.

## No Substantive Changes Needed

The philosophical architecture is intact, the experiential sequence functions as designed, and the TNs are well-placed. TN2's explicit statement of the dissolution thesis is valuable for readers expecting a Camusian resolution.
