## Chinese Philosophy-Specific Observations

### 1. Individualist Starting Point
The key claim "human individuals can only set out from the position of human individuals" (人类个体必然只能从人类个体出发) is rendered clearly. 

**Note for readers:** This represents a philosophical position, not a neutral starting point. In Confucian thought, the self is constituted *through* relationships (五倫) — one doesn't exist as isolated individual first, then extend outward. The author's framing imports Western liberal assumptions. This isn't an error — it's the author's stance — but Chinese readers will notice the departure.

**Recommendation:** No change needed to translation. Consider a TN in the final version if this essay is read before the 道德仁义礼 passage, which makes the author's methodological approach clearer.

### 2. 超越 (Transcendence)
Used correctly throughout. The footnote "it is only by standing upon oneself that transcending oneself can be defined at all" is a key philosophical claim rendered well.

### 3. 人性 Question
The aside "Can human nature (人性), in some sense, also be defined at the macro level?" is preserved appropriately as a question. Good choice to keep it as an aside (blockquote-style) rather than integrated into the argument.

### 4. 良定义 (Well-defined)
"Well-defined" is the correct mathematical borrowing. Preserves the author's move of using formal vocabulary for phenomenological claims.
