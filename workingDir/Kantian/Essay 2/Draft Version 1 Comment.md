## Specific Observations

### 1. The Translator's Note

The TN is well-placed and accurate:
> "The author's use of 'phenomena' throughout this essay corresponds to the Chinese 现象 (*xiànxiàng*) used in the other essays — what we experience or observe, the indubitable ground of all cognition (elsewhere called 'First Reality')."

This provides exactly what English readers need: a bridge to the collection's technical vocabulary. No expansion required.

### 2. Kantian Context (Optional Addition)

The essay's argument goes *further* than Kant:

- **Kant** distinguishes empirical knowledge (possible within the bounds of experience) from transcendent metaphysical knowledge (impossible).
- **The author** collapses the truth condition entirely at the practical level: "The truth condition is methodologically impossible."

This is a more radical deflationary move than Kant's critical limitation. Kant still allows that empirical judgments are *objectively valid* (for all subjects sharing the forms of intuition); the author suggests all knowledge reduces to "what each individual believes to be correct."

**Translation implication:** This radicalism could benefit from a TN noting the relationship to Kant's epistemology — but such a note would be *comparative scholarship*, not translation apparatus. The current minimal approach is correct.

### 3. A Priori Justification (Sidenote)

The sidenote on "A priori justification" is important from a Kantian perspective:
> "A priori justification: justified belief or truth (sometimes phenomena, depending on philosophical position)"

The author leaves the door open for synthetic a priori knowledge but doesn't assert it. This is honest and important. No apparatus needed.

### 4. Phenomena as Starting Point

The essay's core move — that phenomena are the only accessible starting point for justification — parallels Kant's principle that cognition begins with experience. The difference: Kant argues that experience presupposes a priori forms; the author's framework (developed in later essays) leaves this as an empirical question about stable structures (S_E).

This connection becomes clear when reading the collection as a whole. The current TN appropriately signals this without pre-empting the later essays.
