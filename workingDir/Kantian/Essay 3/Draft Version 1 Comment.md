### 3. "Immanent Meaning" (内生的意义)

This rendering deserves attention. The author distinguishes:
- **内生的** (internally generated) — meaning created by the self, but still potentially "directed outward" (entrepreneur, Sisyphus's revolt)
- The cat example goes further — meaning that simply *is*, without articulation or direction

"Immanent" captures this philosophical distinction well: meaning intrinsic to the activity itself, neither imposed from outside nor deliberately constructed from inside. The term echoes Kant's Third Critique — disinterested satisfaction in the beautiful, where we do not *seek* pleasure but simply *find* it.

**No change needed.** The term is philosophically precise.

### 4. The Argument Structure

The essay's progression is preserved:
1. **Sisyphus** — mythic, requires heroic revolt to recover meaning
2. **Office worker** — alienated, meaning externalized
3. **Entrepreneur** — internally generated meaning, but still directed toward "transcendence"
4. **Cat owner** — immanent meaning, absurdity never arises

This structure is load-bearing — it shows the author dissolving (not resolving) the Camusian problematic. TN2 correctly identifies this philosophical move.

### 5. Kantian Resonance

The cat example bypasses both:
- **Kant's practical reason** (meaning grounded in moral duty, the categorical imperative)
- **Existentialist heroism** (meaning affirmed against cosmic indifference)

Instead, it operates closer to Kant's *aesthetic* dimension — the Third Critique's account of pleasure taken in the beautiful without concept or purpose. The cat owner doesn't need to *ground* meaning; they simply *have* it through immediate engagement.

This is not Kant's framework, but it's philosophically coherent and interestingly adjacent. No apparatus needed — the connection is for readers to discover.
