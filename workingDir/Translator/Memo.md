# Memo

## Table of Contents Notes

The collection is titled 境集. Key structural observations:

- **Two-part structure plus appendix:**
  - Part 1: 上集：鱼 (Upper Collection: Fish) — 3 essays, informal/essayistic, each can be read independently
  - Part 2: 下集：渔 (Lower Collection: Fishing) — 4 essays, more systematic/methodological, strongly interlinked, described as working drafts
  - Appendix: 1 essay of scattered thoughts

- **鱼/渔 (Fish/Fishing) metaphor**: This is a deliberate allusion to the classical Chinese proverb 授人以鱼不如授人以渔 ("give a man a fish vs. teach him to fish"). Part 1 gives concrete philosophical reflections (the fish); Part 2 reveals the methodology behind them (the fishing). This structural metaphor is load-bearing and must be preserved in translation.

- **Reading order is prescribed**: The author notes that the essays were written at different times and are NOT arranged chronologically, but the given order is recommended. This confirms that sequencing is philosophically intentional.

- **Part 2 caveat**: The author warns that concepts and terminology differ from established philosophical traditions — "需要注意甄别以免混淆" (one must carefully distinguish to avoid confusion). This is a critical signal for translation: standard philosophical glosses may mislead.

- **Title 境集**: 境 (jing) is a term with enormous range — boundary, realm, situation, state, environment, condition (Buddhist: visaya/gocara). The title itself is a translation challenge. Provisional: leave as romanized "Jing Ji" with a translator's note, or find an English rendering after full reading.

## Essay Reading Notes

### Essay 1: 关于集体利益的一点点思考 其一
**"A Brief Thought on Collective Interest, Part One"**

**Summary:** The essay examines whether "collective interest" (集体利益) genuinely exists or is merely an aggregate of individual interests. The author resolves this by analogy to renormalization (重整化) in physics: just as macroscopic phenomena (ocean waves) emerge from microscopic dynamics (water molecules), collective interest is a well-defined (良定义) emergent phenomenon at a different scale. The essay then argues that individuals can only understand others from their own individual standpoint — this is what makes empathy possible. Laws function like temperature: they act on microscopic individuals but have macroscopic (collective) effects.

**Translation challenges:**
- **重整化 (renormalization)**: A physics term used philosophically. Must retain the technical precision while making the metaphorical extension clear. The author explicitly warns that renormalized phenomena are "genuinely existent" (切实存在) — this is a philosophical claim, not just a physics claim.
- **良定义 (well-defined)**: Mathematical jargon used philosophically. Keep as "well-defined."
- **现象 (phenomenon)**: Used in a quasi-technical sense throughout. Must be consistent with how the later essays use it.
- **有效存在**: "effective existence" — a term borrowed from effective field theory. The author's neologism binds physics methodology to ontology.
- **Register**: The essay moves between colloquial Chinese, physics jargon, and philosophical argument. The tone is exploratory, almost thinking-aloud. Must preserve this informal-yet-rigorous quality.
- **Footnote 1** ("只有立足自身，超越自身才有所定义"): "Only by standing on oneself does transcending oneself become definable." This is a dense philosophical claim compressed into classical-feeling Chinese. The word 立足 (stand on, be grounded in) and 超越 (transcend) need careful treatment.
- **Footnote 2**: Long and important — argues that even altruistic goals ultimately route through the individual's own interest-structure. This is not egoism but a structural claim about phenomenological access.

**Performative structure:** The essay enacts the very move it describes — it begins from the individual's confusion, works outward to the collective, and then returns to the individual. The return is philosophically load-bearing.

### Essay 2: A Thought on Knowledge
**Already in English.** Author: Xueqi Li. Dated June 12, 2025.

**Summary:** A systematic examination of "knowledge as justified true belief" (JTB) through the lens of actual epistemic practice. The author argues: (1) belief is intrinsically subjective and unconnected to objective truth; (2) our access is always to phenomena, not truth directly; (3) justification based on phenomena requires "digestion" into belief, so all justification is ultimately belief-based; (4) this leads to either circular justification or unjustified foundational beliefs; (5) in practice, knowledge is just "what each individual believes to be correct."

**Translation challenges:**
- This essay is already in English, so the main task is harmonization with the Chinese essays. How does 现象 in Essay 1 relate to "phenomena" here? They seem to be the same concept — the author's consistent use of 现象/phenomena across languages is a terminological anchor.
- The term "digestion" (converting phenomena into belief) is informal but philosophically precise. It likely corresponds to something in the Part 2 methodology.
- The sidenotes on physics (Standard Model, particle detection) and mathematics (proof as belief-chain) reinforce the same epistemological point as Essay 1's renormalization metaphor.
- The essay's conclusion — knowledge is "what each individual believes to be correct" — resonates strongly with Essay 1's claim that the individual can only start from and return to itself.

**Performative structure:** The essay methodically walks through each component of JTB, not to refute it abstractly, but to show that *in practice* each component collapses into belief or phenomena. The Bob-and-rain examples are load-bearing — they demonstrate the digestion process.

### Essay 3: 西西弗斯、班与猫
**"Sisyphus, Work, and Cats"** (班 here means going to work/commuting, from 上班)

**Summary:** The essay reinterprets Camus's absurdism. The author's core claim: absurdity arises only when one seeks meaning externally (from the universe). When meaning is internally generated, absurdity never arises. Three cases demonstrate this: (1) Sisyphus — canonical absurdist figure, but his "revolt" is itself an internal meaning; (2) the office worker — alienated labor creates absurdity because meaning is externalized; the same work done as one's own project (创业) dissolves absurdity; (3) cat ownership — involves repetitive, laborious, Sisyphean tasks (cleaning litter, dealing with fur), yet NO ONE who owns a cat ever feels absurd about it. The cat example is decisive: it shows that meaning does not require transcendence, rebellion, or even articulation — it is simply there, immanent in the activity itself.

**Translation challenges:**
- **Title**: 班 is colloquial for work/commuting (from 上班). "Sisyphus, Work, and Cats" captures it, but loses the sonic/graphic parallel of the three monosyllables 西西弗斯、班、猫. Consider: "Sisyphus, the Office, and the Cat."
- **荒谬 (absurdity)**: Standard Camus translation term. Must be consistent.
- **非和谐 (off-tone/disharmony)**: The author's own coinage (with English gloss "off-tone" in the original). This is a key concept — the felt discord between the self seeking meaning and the universe's silence.
- **异化 (alienation)**: Marxist resonance. The author ties absurdity to alienation — meaning externalized into labor.
- **超越 (transcendence)**: Used in scare quotes by the author. Gods and the universe are "transcendent" external existences. Internal meaning does not require transcendence.
- **内生 (endogenous/internally generated)**: Economics/biology term used philosophically. "Endogenous meaning" vs "exogenous meaning" could work, but may over-technicalize.

**Performative structure:** The essay is brilliantly sequenced. It moves from the most dramatic (Sisyphus vs. the gods) through the everyday dramatic (office alienation) to the utterly mundane (cat ownership). Each step strips away a layer of grandeur. The final example is philosophically decisive precisely because it is so ordinary — no revolt, no transcendence, just a cat. The reader's own recognition ("of course no one feels absurd about owning a cat") is the philosophical payload. This sequencing MUST be preserved; it cannot be rearranged.

1. **Individual as starting point**: All three essays begin from and return to the individual. Essay 1: the individual can only understand from its own standpoint. Essay 2: knowledge is what the individual believes. Essay 3: meaning is internally generated. This is a consistent phenomenological commitment.

4. **Register**: Essays 1 and 3 are in Chinese with a thinking-aloud, exploratory register. Essay 2 is in English with a more formal, analytic-philosophy register. The collection deliberately includes both languages. Translation must decide: translate Essay 2 into Chinese? Or is the bilingual nature of the collection itself significant?

5. **Progression**: Essay 1 (ontology of collective vs. individual) -> Essay 2 (epistemology: what can the individual know?) -> Essay 3 (existential: what about meaning?). The progression from ontology to epistemology to existential meaning is deliberate.

### Essay 4: 后形而上学 整理
**"Post-Metaphysics: A Compilation"**

**Summary:** The methodological core of Part 2. The author develops a systematic framework: (1) Pre-metaphysics (前形而上学) = traditional metaphysics before nihilism; (2) Phenomena as First Reality (第一实在) — the indubitable starting point; (3) Four types of metaphysical construction (形而上), each involving recognition of repetition (恒常) then fixation into concept; (4) The Laozi example showing how 道-德-仁-义-礼 is generated in reverse from how it is understood; (5) Non-rationality (非理性) as the acknowledged ground of rational activity; (6) Post-metaphysics: grounded in phenomena, core practice = overlap of ideal and phenomena = practice = self-realization.

**Translation challenges:**
- **前/后形而上学**: Author's coinages. Must NOT be confused with Habermas's "post-metaphysical."
- **第一实在/第二实在/第〇实在**: Numbered reality levels (First/Second/Zeroth Reality). Author's own system.
- **恒常**: "Constancy" — the fundamental drive behind all metaphysical construction.
- **The Laozi passage**: Romanize (Dao, De, Ren, Yi, Li) with glosses. 现象礼/现象仁/现象义 = "phenomenal-Li/Ren/Yi" (neologisms).
- **非理性**: "Non-rational" (not "irrational").
- **形而上 vs. 形而上学**: "The metaphysical" vs. "metaphysics" — distinction is load-bearing.

**Performative structure:** Constructs a framework, shows how the framework is constructed from phenomena, acknowledges the non-rationality of that constructive activity.

### Essay 5: 形而上学后的目的：诗意
**"The Purpose After Metaphysics: The Poetic"**

**Summary:** Introduces 梦境 (dreamscape) as the environment in which meaning exists. Two modes: 沉醉的 (intoxicated — faith, meaning as unquestioned first principle) and 清醒的 (lucid — knowing engagement, = 诗意/the poetic). We cannot reject all dreamscapes because we are constituted by feeling. After disenchantment, the only path is lucid engagement: poiesis.

**Translation challenges:**
- **诗意**: THE central challenge. Not "poetic" or "poetry." A mode of being: lucid, knowing engagement with meaning-structures. Options: "the poetic," "poiesis," "poetic dwelling." Must consult experts.
- **梦境**: "Dreamscape" — spatial/environmental quality.
- **沉醉/清醒**: "Intoxicated/Lucid."
- **虚妄**: Author's personal coinage (empty + presumptuous).
- **感受 and 意义 as 两面一体**: Feeling and meaning as two sides of one body.

### Phenomenology Expert — Key Translation Insights

1. **P as Lebenswelt**: The totality of phenomena functions like Husserl's pre-theoretical lifeworld. P is the "phenomenological field" given formal character.
2. **Tower structure = infinite iterability of reflection**: Morphisms becoming objects mirrors phenomenological insight that acts of consciousness can become objects of new acts recursively.
3. **Universal properties = eidetic constitution**: The concept of "pen" as terminal object in a subcategory formalizes Husserl's eidetic constitution — how essences are constituted through synthesis of particulars.
4. **Ship of Theseus = noematic nucleus**: The feeling that serves as universal property linking associated experiences is precisely Husserl's noematic Kern.
5. **感受 (ganshou)**: "Feeling" or "felt experience" — carries weight of both Husserl's Empfindung and Michel Henry's affection. NOT mere "sensation."
6. **体验 (tiyan)**: "Lived experience" — Chinese equivalent of Erlebnis.
7. **E-notation**: Author's own E = Enclosed/Emotion/Experience. Expert suggests "Enclosure" as possible rendering but notes it loses Chinese resonances.
8. **Introspection as phenomenological method**: Using structural relations as a "map" to navigate experience = close to Husserl's free variation.
9. **eta = fulfilled intention (erfullte Intention)**: When eta is identity, meaning-intention is perfectly filled by the given object.
10. **Intersubjectivity via eta**: Sidesteps qualia problem entirely. Two people share understanding when structural alignment has the same form. Improves on Husserl's appresentation.
11. **Bootstrapping problem remains**: How do two E-structures become coordinated through interaction? This is the residual question.

6. **Access argument**: The shift from "what justification bases are" to "how we access them" is the essay's most important move. "Methodological phenomenalism" applied to epistemology.
7. **Bob examples are load-bearing**: Three Bobs must appear in exact sequence with specific details (sprinkler, emotional absorption). They are a philosophical experiment.

### Existentialism Expert — Key Translation Insights

1. **荒谬 (huangmiu)**: "Absurdity" — but Chinese term carries stronger "ridiculousness" connotation than French absurde. This actually serves the argument: the absurd IS ridiculous because it's a misdirected search.
2. **虚无 (xuwu)**: "The void" (substantive) / "nihilism" (虚无主义). Chinese term has Daoist cosmological resonance (productive void) absent from Nietzsche's Nihilismus. Translator's note needed.
3. **诗意**: Expert recommends "the poetic" with "poetic dwelling" in habitative contexts. Needs translator's essay explaining it functions like Heidegger's Dichtung — world-disclosing activity, not versification.
4. **超越/超越性**: "Transcendence" — but used in 3 senses: (a) beyond self, (b) beyond phenomena, (c) outward-reaching quality of goals. Annotation needed.
5. **Author's 诗意 vs. Western alternatives**: More livable than Nietzsche's amor fati (too ecstatic), Camus's revolt (too heroic), Heidegger's Gelassenheit (too quietist). It is active but not heroic, aware but not detached.
6. **Cat example**: Will likely become a memorable philosophical example in its own right. The mundane is philosophically decisive precisely because it is ordinary.

8. **Coin-flip as 诗意**: Compact example of lucid engagement — you must genuinely invest in the constructed 境 for it to work.

### Philosophy of Mind Expert — Key Translation Insights

1. **E as cognitive architecture**: Category theory captures composability and hierarchy of mental operations, not just static structure. Morphism composition = coherent chains of association.
2. **Arrows-only metacategory**: No fundamental distinction between "a concept" and "an association between concepts." Resonates with William James's radical empiricism.
3. **Tower structure = meta-cognition**: T_1 = thinking about thinking. Flattening property = meta-cognitive levels coexist in unified field (matching phenomenological observation).
4. **Meta-order is non-intrinsic**: Dragon blood example — which level of abstraction something is at depends on cognitive history. No direct analogue in standard Higher-Order Thought theories.
5. **S_E as a priori cognitive structures**: Parallels Kant's categories, Chomsky's Universal Grammar, Spelke's core knowledge. Framework contribution: characterizes these as structural invariants under categorical evolution.
6. **Basket metaphor (篮子)**: Captures cognitive closure (McGinn) — what hasn't entered P is unavailable for processing. Transforms metaphysical limit into empirical research program.
7. **eta as "feeling of knowing"**: Formally precise phenomenological characterization of understanding. When eta is identity = "mastery" (掌握). Naturality condition = "transfer" in education.
8. **联想 (lianxiang)**: "Association" — but richer. Classical associationism (Hume) upgraded with composability and identity. Closer to "cognitive morphism."
9. **Universal properties as "the feeling of a concept"**: Not a definition or prototype but a quale — the characteristic feeling of pen-ness functioning as terminal object.
10. **Intersubjectivity**: Subsumes Zahavi's empathy, Gallese's shared manifold, Brandom's inferentialism as special cases.

### Kantian Expert — Key Translation Insights

1. **Author's 第一实在 is pre-categorical**: Closer to Kant's "manifold of intuition" before synthesis, or even sensory "matter" of appearance, than to Kantian Erscheinung (always already constituted). More Husserlian than Kantian.
2. **S_E parallels Kantian categories**: But author leaves S_E unspecified (to be discovered empirically), unlike Kant who deduces from table of judgments. Author's approach is more neo-Kantian.
3. **"Another kind of Kantian question"**: Author explicitly frames "How is meaning possible?" as parallel to "How is synthetic a priori knowledge possible?" — extending Kantian architecture to meaning/value.
4. **Category theory vs. table of categories**: Universal properties replace Kantian categories, functors replace schematism, natural transformations replace synthetic unity of apperception. Genuine formalization of what Kant gestured at.
5. **Dynamic vs. static**: Author introduces evolution functor T, making experience fundamentally dynamic. Kant's framework is static — categories don't change.
6. **eta addresses Kant's intersubjectivity gap**: Kant assumes all rational beings share same cognitive architecture but never proves it. Author's eta provides a structural, testable criterion.
7. **Non-rationality divergence**: Kant would never call the drive for metaphysics "non-rational" — for him, reason's demand for the unconditioned IS rational.
8. **形而上 reinterpretation**: Author reads 形 in 形而上者谓之道 as 现象 (phenomena), not the traditional "visible form." This is a significant hermeneutical move that should be noted.

### Chinese Philosophy Expert — Key Translation Insights

1. **道德仁义礼 reversal**: No Chinese philosopher has explicitly argued the entire hierarchy is a phenomenological construction built bottom-up. Closest precedent is Xunzi's empiricism about 礼, but Xunzi never extended to 道 itself.
2. **德 (de)**: "Inherent character" or retain "De." NOT "virtue" (imports moral connotations author strips away). NOT "essence" (imports Western metaphysical baggage being deconstructed). Author's constructivist spin: 德 is assembled from inductively generalized properties then normatively imposed as definitional.
3. **仁 (ren)**: "Humaneness" or "fellow-feeling" — author emphasizes inner feeling first, behavioral expression second.
4. **义 (yi)**: "Moral impetus" or "sense of obligation" — author emphasizes motivational bridge quality.
5. **礼 (li)**: "Ritual conduct" or "patterned behavior" at phenomenal level. NOT religious ceremony.
6. **境 as Chinese concept**: Author preserves core Buddhist insight that 境 is structured by mind, not "the world out there." But transforms: formalized via category theory, given dynamic evolution. Closest traditional resonance is 境界 in its most general sense.
7. **诗意 as synthesis**: Chinese aesthetic tradition (意境) + Buddhist awareness (觉/悟) + Western post-nihilist existentialism. Rooted in Chinese sensibility but not confined by it.
8. **恒常 vs. Daoist 常 vs. Buddhist 无常**: Author's 恒常 is epistemological/pragmatic — what we seek in phenomena. Not ontological like Daoist 常 (eternal pattern) or Buddhist 无常 (no permanence).
9. **虚无 vs. Buddhist 空**: Completely different. Author's 虚无 = absence of transcendent ground. Buddhist 空 = dependent origination. Must NOT be conflated.
10. **Essays as experiments**: Parallels classical Chinese method — Mencius's 孺子入井, Zhuangzi's parables. The form IS the method. Translation must preserve experiential arc.
11. **Double burden of every key term**: Traditional resonance + author's reinterpretation. Translator must preserve both.

### Buddhism Expert — Key Translation Insights

1. **境 and Yogacara visaya**: Author preserves insight that 境 is domain as structured by mind, not "world out there." But enriches: formalizes relations between object-takings, not just object-takings themselves.
2. **Tower structure resonates with Hua-yen (华严)**: Indra's net — mutual reflection, tower-like structure. Fa-zang's metaphor is the single best Buddhist image for what the tower construction formalizes.
3. **Post-metaphysics = Middle Way**: Pre-metaphysics (eternalism/常见) -> nihilism (断见) -> post-metaphysics (middle way). Remarkably precise structural parallel.
4. **清醒的梦境 parallels Chan awakening**: "Mountains are mountains again" — third stage. Full engagement with full awareness. Diamond Sutra: "contemplate them thus while continuing practice."
5. **Self as universal property = anatman**: "I" as terminal object in subcategory, not substance = Middle Way on self. Neither eternalism nor nihilism.
6. **恒常 = first cognitive inversion (viparyasa)**: Taking impermanent as permanent. Author's analysis parallels Buddhist diagnosis precisely.
7. **Critical difference from Buddhism**: Author proposes "post-metaphysical constancy-seeking" (recognize and work with it lucidly) rather than "cessation of constancy-seeking" (overcome through insight).
8. **清醒 carries overtones of 觉 (bodhi)**: "Lucid" best preserves both clear-seeing and dreamscape context. Avoid "awakened" (too Buddhist) and "conscious" (too Cartesian).
9. **Author's 虚无 is NOT sunyata**: Kumarajiva rendered sunyata as 空 precisely to avoid confusion with 無. Author follows this distinction implicitly. Must be noted.
10. **Philosophical experiments = upaya (skillful means)**: Buddhist pedagogical tradition treats teachings as devices for producing experiential transformations. Koans are the purest example.

### Wittgenstein Expert — Key Translation Insights

1. **Language games reoriented**: Author treats language games as probes (methodological instruments), not as bedrock (Wittgenstein's use). Closer to Brandom's appropriation — taking primacy of practice and embedding in systematic project.
2. **Structure/meaning resolution via natural transformation**: Neither generates the other; they must be aligned. Formalizes Wittgenstein's meaning-as-use insight that meaning and use are co-constitutive.
3. **Natural transformation subsumes both picture theory AND meaning-as-use**: Picture theory as special case (eta = isomorphism); later Wittgenstein as general case (eta = experiential alignment).
4. **Philosophical therapy explained structurally**: Tension (张力) = subcategory lacking well-defined universal property. Therapy = restructuring category to resolve tension. Wittgenstein would resist the explanation but the account provides positive criterion for when puzzlement arises.
5. **理解 vs. 掌握**: "Understanding" vs. "mastery/direct grasp." 掌握 = eta is identity morphism = no mediation. Important distinction for translation.
6. **Private language argument compatibility**: Author's structural position criterion IS the public criterion Wittgenstein argues sensation-terms must have. Beetle-in-the-box: private content drops out; structural role remains.
7. **On Certainty parallels**: Author's analysis and OC arrive at similar conclusions. But author dissolves knowledge/belief distinction entirely (more pragmatist); Wittgenstein preserves the grammar via hinge propositions.
8. **Forms of life vs. 境**: Parallel but not identical. Forms of life are communal background; 境 is individual structural medium. Different starting points, overlapping territory.
9. **Kripkenstein problem addressed**: Naturality condition constrains eta globally, not just at finitely many points — no room for quaddition-like deviance.
10. **Essays as experiments**: Most Wittgensteinian aspect — philosophy as activity, not doctrine. Medium is the message.

### 3. Register Strategy

The collection moves between at least 5 registers. The translation must preserve these shifts:

1. **Colloquial/thinking-aloud** (Essays 1, 3, parts of 8): Informal, exploratory, sometimes humorous. Use natural conversational English. Preserve run-on thoughts, self-corrections, parenthetical asides.

2. **Analytic philosophy** (Essay 2, parts of 4): Precise, argumentative, uses technical terms (JTB, justification, phenomena). Already in English for Essay 2 — use as the register standard.

3. **Classical Chinese quotation** (Laozi, Mengzi, Yijing in Essays 4, 8): Romanize and gloss. Provide standard English translations of classical passages, with notes on how the author departs from standard readings.

4. **Mathematical/formal** (Essays 6, 7): Standard mathematical English. Preserve all notation. Category theory terms use established English conventions (functor, morphism, natural transformation, universal property, etc.).

5. **Near-lyrical/poetic** (Essay 5 climax, Essay 8 conclusion): Rare in this collection, which makes these moments load-bearing. Preserve the shift to elevated register.

### 4. Classical Chinese Quotation Format

**Format for 文言 (classical Chinese) quotations:**

1. Present original 文言 text as a **block quote**
2. Follow with **glossed English translation** — key terms glossed on first occurrence as "English (漢字, pinyin)", e.g., "humaneness (仁, ren)"
3. After first introduction, use **plain English only** — e.g., just "humaneness"
4. **现象X compounds** (现象仁, 现象礼, 现象义) keep the "phenomenal" prefix **always** — never drop it. The distinction between "humaneness" (classical concept) and "phenomenal humaneness" (author's coinage for lived experience) is load-bearing for the argument.

**Refinements:**
- Re-gloss briefly at **register transitions** — when the same term (e.g., 仁) shifts from classical quotation to the author's modern analysis, add a brief "(仁)" reminder
- Only gloss **philosophically load-bearing terms**, not every content word
- For the author's coined compounds (现象仁 etc.), no romanization needed after first gloss — "phenomenal humaneness" is self-explanatory
- Make the 文言/modern Chinese boundary visible through **formatting** (block-indent for classical quotations vs. running text for the author's analysis)

*Unanimously endorsed by all 9 experts.*

### 5. Structural Principles

1. **Preserve experiential sequences exactly**. Every expert independently flagged this. The Sisyphus-to-cat progression, the Dao-De-Ren-Yi-Li reversal, the three Bobs, the 1+1=2 passage — these are philosophical experiments. Do not reorder, summarize, or abstract.

2. **Preserve working-draft quality**. Todo sections, self-corrections, incomplete passages, and authorial hedges ("I think," "perhaps") are part of the philosophical honesty of the collection. Do not polish them away.

3. **Preserve the bilingual nature**. Essay 2 is in English. The collection is deliberately bilingual. The Chinese-to-English translation encompasses 7 essays + table of contents. Essay 2 stays as-is (with minor harmonization of terminology if needed).

4. **Preserve the fish/fishing structure**. Part 1 (上集：鱼) and Part 2 (下集：渔) must retain the structural metaphor. Translate as "Upper Collection: Fish" and "Lower Collection: Fishing" (or similar), with a translator's note on the proverb 授人以鱼不如授人以渔.

5. **Footnotes and sidenotes**: Author's footnotes are often philosophically essential (e.g., Essay 1 footnote 2, Essay 4 footnote 25). Translate all footnotes. Add translator's notes sparingly — only where (a) a Chinese cultural reference would be opaque to English readers, (b) a Western philosophical parallel would help, or (c) the internet meme requires explanation.
