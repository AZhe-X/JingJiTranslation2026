### Strengths

1. **"Well-defined" (良定义):** Correct choice. The author's question "can X be well-defined at the macro level?" is asking about conditions of meaningful reference — when can we sensibly *talk about* something. This is a language-use question disguised as ontology.

2. **"Effective existence" (有效存在):** The TN correctly explains the physics sense ("genuinely operative at that scale"). This prevents misreading as "merely apparent" — the author's point is that emergence doesn't diminish reality.

3. **"Cognized as a whole" (当作一个整体来认知):** Preserves the key insight that reference-level matters. We can meaningfully refer to "the ocean's motion pattern" even though it emerges from molecules. This is Wittgenstein-adjacent: the meaning of "collective interest" depends on the language-game in which it functions, not reduction to micro-constituents.

4. **Exploratory tone preserved:** The first-person, diary-like quality ("Today I have some thoughts...") is retained. This matters because the essay performs philosophy-as-reflection, not philosophy-as-treatise.
