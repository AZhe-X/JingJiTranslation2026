#### EDIT 2: Line 50 — the structural claims paragraph

**CURRENT:**
"Two structural claims of the collection deserve advance notice. First, the author treats nihilism not as the negation of metaphysics but as its *consequence*: when metaphysical systems claim to describe ultimate reality and then fail, the void they leave is nihilism. Post-metaphysics is the path that emerges after recognizing this -- not by rejecting metaphysical construction, but by practicing it lucidly. Second, the author treats absurdity not as a permanent human condition (as in Camus) but as a structural collapse: absurdity arises when one's Jing seeks meaning externally and finds none. The dissolution of absurdity -- demonstrated in Essay 3 through the progression from Sisyphus to cat ownership -- occurs not through revolt but through inhabiting a Jing in which meaning is internally generated."

**FINAL:**
"The three essays of Part 1 examine, respectively, collective interest, knowledge, and absurdity -- each through a distinctive philosophical method the author does not yet name. The four essays of Part 2 develop the formal framework behind that method: the concepts of the metaphysical, constancy, the poetic, and a categorical formalism for the structure of experience. The relationship between the two parts is itself part of the argument."

**Decision:** Majority (8 vs 2). Full removal of both structural claims. Topic-only replacement names what the essays examine without revealing what they conclude. The final sentence signals that the Fish/Fishing structure is load-bearing without saying how. All removed content moves to Afterword Section A.

**Minority position noted:** Two team members (Wittgenstein expert, Translator-2) preferred retaining a minimal directional hint ("consequence rather than negation"). The majority judged that even this partial framing pre-interprets the reader's encounter with Essay 4.

#### EDIT 3: Lines 95-96 — the Essay 3 example in "Experiential Sequences"

**CURRENT:**
"In Essay 3, the author arranges three cases of repetitive, apparently meaningless labor in a specific order: Sisyphus rolling his boulder, an office worker commuting to an alienating job, a cat owner cleaning the litter box. The progression from mythic to mundane is the philosophical payload. By the third example, the reader recognizes -- from their own experience -- that no one who owns a cat has ever felt absurd about it. This recognition, which cannot be produced by argument alone, is the essay's central philosophical move. The sequence cannot be rearranged, summarized, or abstracted without destroying it."

**FINAL:**
"In Essay 3, the author arranges three cases of repetitive, apparently meaningless labor in a specific order, progressing from the mythic to the mundane. The progression itself is the philosophical payload: by the final example, the reader is invited to test the essay's claim against their own lived experience. The recognition that follows -- which cannot be produced by argument alone -- is the essay's central philosophical move. The sequence cannot be rearranged, summarized, or abstracted without destroying it."

**Decision:** Majority. Removes: (a) the specific listing of the three cases, (b) the punchline, (c) the mechanism. Preserves: (a) three-case structure, (b) mythic-to-mundane direction, (c) experiential-experiment principle, (d) sequence-integrity warning. The section now practices what it preaches — it describes the method without abstracting the experience.

**Minority position noted:** One team member (Chinese-phil expert) preferred keeping the specific listing (Sisyphus, office worker, cat owner) while removing the punchline. The majority judged that listing the cases slightly pre-loads the reader's attention and that the essay title ("Sisyphus, Work, and Cats") already provides sufficient teasing.

### PART 2: TRANSLATOR'S AFTERWORD — Blueprint

#### Placement
After all essays and the Appendix, before the Glossary Introduction.

Reading order: Translator's Preface -> Essays 1-7 -> Appendix (Essay 8) -> **Translator's Afterword** -> Glossary Introduction -> Glossary.

#### Tone
Scholarly but accessible. The translators speaking as philosophically informed readers. More analytically engaged than the Preface. Not evaluative or promotional. Should model the cross-traditional reading the work invites. The closing should return the reader to their own experience rather than ending on scholarly commentary.

#### Length
3,000-4,000 words.

#### Structure

**Section A: The Collection's Philosophical Argument (~800 words)**

Now that the reader has experienced the essays, we name what they demonstrate:

- The structural claims removed from the Preface, stated freely and expanded:
  - Nihilism as consequence of metaphysics (not its negation)
  - Absurdity as structural Jing-collapse that dissolves when meaning is internally generated
  - The bottom-up construction of the metaphysical from phenomena
- The full progression: traditional metaphysics -> nihilism -> post-metaphysics -> the poetic
- The Essay 3 punchline and its significance, discussed openly
- The Dao-De reversal's implications: how bottom-up construction is concealed and appears top-down
- The knowledge-as-belief thesis and its relationship to the formal apparatus
- The collection as its own philosophical experiment: the reader who has completed it has undergone a structured experiential sequence that demonstrates the very concepts the essays describe

**Section B: Cross-Traditional Connections (~1,200-1,500 words)**

The Afterword's distinctive contribution — scholarly positioning that exists nowhere else in the volume. Framed as **convergences, not influences** — the author arrives at these positions independently, which is itself philosophically significant.

- **Kantian parallels:** The meaning question as "another kind of Kantian question" (the author's own phrase). S_E as conditions of possible experience. The departure from Kant's fixed transcendental apparatus toward an evolving, constructive one. The intersubjectivity solution via natural transformation eta. **The dispensability of Zeroth Reality (第〇实在) vs. Kant's Ding an sich as necessary limiting concept — the author's Zeroth Reality loses its special position entirely through disenchantment (祛魅), enabling the post-metaphysical turn.**
- **Phenomenological connections:** Phenomena as starting point (cf. Husserl's Lebenswelt). The relationship between Jing and intentional constitution. Departures from Heidegger's ontological project. The author's greater agentiveness compared to Heidegger's poetic dwelling.
- **Existentialist positioning:** The Poetic compared with Nietzsche's amor fati, Camus's revolt, Heidegger's Gelassenheit — and how it differs from all three. **The "livability" of the author's post-nihilistic stance: the cat-raiser, the coin-flipper, the ritual-performer as figures of everyday life, not philosophical heroes — contrasting with Nietzsche's Ubermensch, Camus's absurd hero, and Kierkegaard's knight of faith. The intoxicated/lucid dreamscape distinction as resolution of the bad faith/authenticity problematic.**
- **Buddhist structural parallels:** Madhyamaka middle way (pre-metaphysics/nihilism/post-metaphysics paralleling eternalism/nihilism/middle way). Yogacara visaya and the constructed object-domain. Chan awakening stages ("mountains are mountains again"). The Diamond Sutra dream-simile and 梦境. **Hua-yen's Indra's net and the tower structure. Pratityasamutpada as relational constitution.**
- **Wittgensteinian resonances:** Language games and forms of life. Showing vs. saying. The therapeutic method and its relationship to the author's philosophical experiments. **The natural transformation eta as giving mathematical content to Wittgenstein's beetle-in-the-box insight. A structural (rather than communitarian) solution to the rule-following problem.**
- **Chinese philosophical tradition:** The radical reinterpretation of 形, 德, and the Dao-De hierarchy. The relationship to Neo-Confucian li/qi discussions and Wang Yangming's 知行合一. The 意境 aesthetic tradition. The Zhuangzi's 庄周梦蝶. How this work departs from both Confucian and Daoist orthodoxy. **Relationship to the commentarial tradition (Heshang Gong, Wang Bi) and Xunzi's 礼论.**
- **Philosophy of Mind:** The natural transformation (eta) model of understanding as the framework's most significant formal innovation. **The tower structure as a model of meta-cognition. The "philosophical experiment" methodology as parallel to Varela's neurophenomenology. The intersubjectivity solution via shared eta-structure (structural commensurability without shared qualia).**

**Section C: The Experiential Method Revisited (~500 words)**

The full descriptions softened in the Preface, now stated openly:
- The Essay 3 sequence analyzed in detail: why the cat example works, how the progression from externalized to internally generated meaning demonstrates that absurdity is structural, not existential
- The significance of the method: philosophical experiments that construct a Jing for the reader
- Precedents in Mencius (孺子入井), Zhuangzi's parables, Chan 转语 (turning words), Wittgenstein's therapeutic method
- The author's category-theoretic formalization of experiential method as genuinely novel
- The self-referential dimension: the collection itself is a philosophical experiment on the reader
- **The "two questions" from the Appendix:** "How is philosophy possible?" and "How is meaning possible?" as the collection's guiding questions, with the second connected to Kant's question about synthetic a priori knowledge

**Section D: Translation as Philosophical Practice (~400 words)**

Key translation decisions that embody philosophical arguments:
- The 德 virtue/character split as enactment of the author's argument
- The "phenomenal X" apparatus and its philosophical motivation
- The meme substitution in Essay 7 (experiential fidelity over literal accuracy)
- The choice to leave 境 untranslated
- The principle stated in the Preface — "the experience *is* the argument" — as the governing translation philosophy
- What was lost and what was gained in the crossing between languages

**Section E: What Remains Open (~300 words)**

Honest acknowledgment of the collection's incompleteness and open questions:
- The working-draft character of the Part 2 essays (todo markers, the KA conjecture)
- The question of whether stable structures S_E have normative implications
- The relationship to other formal frameworks (HoTT, topos theory)
- The question of cultural relativity of stable structures
- The underdeveloped treatment of temporality (inner time-consciousness not yet captured by the discrete evolution functor) and embodiment
- The bootstrapping problem (how do two E-structures become coordinated?)
- **An honest note on limits:** the framework's individualist starting point may miss the communal character of meaning; the mathematical apparatus may create new confusions even as it resolves old ones

**Closing (~100 words)**

Return the reader to their own experience. Echo the author's closing gesture in the Appendix: we are all already constructing Jing in our daily lives. The best way to test this framework is not to evaluate its formal apparatus but to notice, in the course of an ordinary day, whether you recognize the structures it describes.

**Optional addition: Suggestions for further reading** — brief pointers to Husserl's *Crisis*, Heidegger's "Building Dwelling Thinking," Wittgenstein's *Philosophical Investigations*, the Diamond Sutra, Mencius, and the *Daodejing*.
