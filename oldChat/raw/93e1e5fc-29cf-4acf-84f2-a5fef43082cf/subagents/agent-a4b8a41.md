The key difference between Heidegger's "poetic dwelling" (*dichterisch wohnen*) and the author's 诗意 (the Poetic) lies in their starting points and the direction of agency.

**Heidegger's poetic dwelling** is fundamentally receptive. In "...Poetically Man Dwells..." and the later essays, Heidegger's poet receives — Being discloses itself through language, and the poet's task is to attend to that disclosure. The poet does not construct meaning; the poet listens. Poetic dwelling is grounded in *Gelassenheit* (releasement, letting-be) — a meditative openness to what shows itself. The source of meaning is ontological: it comes from Being, through language, to the human who is capable of hearing it.

**The author's 诗意** is fundamentally constructive. The author arrives at 诗意 not through meditation on language and Being but through the *exhaustion of metaphysics* — after recognizing that all metaphysical frameworks are built bottom-up from phenomena, and that nihilism is what remains when this is acknowledged. The Poetic is then the stance of actively constructing and inhabiting meaning-structures (境/Jing) while knowing they are constructed. The source of meaning is phenomenological and agentive: it comes from the subject's own experiential field, through the associations and structures the subject builds upon phenomena.

Three specific differences worth noting:

1. **Disclosure vs. construction.** Heidegger's poet discovers meaning that is already there, waiting to be heard. The author's poetic agent constructs meaning that was not there before — the dreamscape (梦境) is built, not found. The ritual examples in Essay 5 make this vivid: burning incense to convey grief, performing ceremonies to show importance. These are *made*, not *received*.

2. **Language vs. phenomena.** Heidegger privileges language as the "house of Being" — poetic dwelling happens in and through language. The author's framework is broader: language (E_L) is one sub-category of the experiential field (E), alongside mathematics (E_M), embodied practice, and others. The Poetic can be practiced through any medium that constructs a Jing — games, music, arranging one's desk, raising a cat. Language has no ontological priority.

3. **The lucidity condition.** Heidegger does not frame poetic dwelling as requiring awareness that one's meaning-structures are constructed. If anything, Heidegger's later thought moves *away* from the subject's self-awareness and toward a kind of surrender to Being's self-showing. The author insists on lucidity (清醒) as constitutive of the Poetic — the distinction between intoxicated and lucid dreamscapes is the entire point. Without knowing that the dreamscape is a dreamscape, you have faith or belief (沉醉), not the Poetic. This lucidity condition has no real analogue in Heidegger.

The convergence is real — both arrive at a non-instrumental, non-technological relationship to meaning after the failure of metaphysics. But the convergence is *convergent*, not genealogical. The author does not build on Heidegger and likely would resist the ontological framework that makes Heidegger's version possible. The author's version is, in my assessment, more livable precisely because it does not require the quasi-mystical receptivity that Heidegger's later thought demands — it asks only that you do what you are already doing (constructing meaning-structures in daily life) with awareness that you are doing it.
