**1. "Rightness" still appears in the block quote (line 34) — NEEDS FIX.**

We decided: "righteousness" in the block quote (loaded, conventional) → "rightness" in the author's analysis (deflated, reconceptualized). This mirrors the virtue → character shift. But the draft uses "rightness" everywhere, including the block quote. Line 34 should read:

**2. Line 40: Consider signaling the virtue → character shift in running text, not just TN3.**

Original: "我们便把我们认为（应当是）人人皆而有之的东西，在这里便是'现象仁'，称之为'德'"

This is correct, but the shift from "virtue" (block quote) to "character" (analysis) happens here without explicit marking in the text itself. TN3 is attached, which helps. But consider adding a brief in-text signal:

"...and call it 'character' (德, *de*) — what appeared as 'virtue' in the classical formulation."

This makes the reconceptualization visible for readers who skip footnotes. Optional — TN3 does the work — but it would strengthen the philosophical experience.

**3. Footnote [^3]: "what ought to be" rather than "what one ought to do" for 义/应当.**

Original: "所谓义，是谓应当"

The draft renders this as "what one ought to do." But 应当 is just "ought" or "what ought to be" — broader than "what one ought to do" (which implies action). 义 in this context is the *sense of what ought to be*, which then becomes the motivational bridge to action. Consider: "rightness (义) is said to be *what ought to be*" — preserving the broader scope before the author narrows it to motivation in the following sentences.

**4. Line 44: "general pattern" → consider "general law" or "universal regularity" for 规律.**

Original: "德是一种一般的规律，因为凡事诸物皆有其德（我们认为他应该遵守的规律，使得一样东西成为其本身的东西）"

"General pattern" is acceptable for 一般的规律, but "pattern" may be too weak. 规律 means "law, regularity, pattern" — it carries normative force. The parenthetical says "the pattern we take them to *follow*" (应该遵守的规律), which implies something more binding than a pattern. Consider "general law" or "universal regularity" to convey that 德 is not just a pattern we observe but a regularity we take things to follow.
