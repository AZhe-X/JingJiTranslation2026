## Claude at 2026-02-11 03:56:56

Analysis complete and sent to team lead. Here is a summary of what I found:

**Key finding:** The author explicitly distinguishes two senses of 感受 in Essay 5, footnote [^2]: (1) received sensory input (closer to 感觉/sensation), and (2) the "crystallization, surging, turbulence" that constitutes meaningful felt awareness. The author uses 感受 as the fundamental unit of phenomenal reality -- "the totality of 感受 is phenomena" (Essay 6).

**The author's implicit taxonomy across the corpus:**
- **感觉** (ganjue) = raw sensation (rare)
- **感受** (ganshou) = fundamental unit of phenomena, both input and felt meaning (primary term)
- **体验** (tiyan) = structured experience in designed contexts (games, art, experiments)
- **经验** (jingyan) = empirical experience in the epistemological sense

**My recommendation: "felt experience"** for 感受 as the default rendering. It preserves the 感 (felt, first-person) quality while maintaining the breadth of "experience." It distinguishes 感受 from 体验 ("experience") and 经验 ("empirical experience"). Context-sensitive alternatives: "feeling" for the raw-input sense, "experience and feeling" when paired with 体验.
