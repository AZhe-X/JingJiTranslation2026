**CONCERNS — things to reconsider:**

1. **"Generates" (生) in "The Way generates all things."** The author is about to deconstruct this very claim — showing that generation runs in the opposite direction. Consider scare quotes on "generates" to signal the author is presenting the received view, not endorsing it. Suggestion, not insistence.

2. **"The most 'primordial' metaphysics" for 最"元始"的元物理.** The original uses 元物理 (meta-physics), the author's own decomposed gloss, NOT the standard 形而上学. The earlier paragraph (line 16 of the original) already introduced this: "metaphysics literally means 元物理 (meta-physics)." When the author repeats 元物理 here, it is deliberate. Consider rendering as "*meta-physics*" (hyphenated) to preserve the etymological self-consciousness. This matters because the author's argument hinges on the difference between 形而上 (above-the-phenomenal) and 元物理 (meta-physics) as two readings of the same concept.

3. **"Character" at first analysis occurrence (line 40).** The draft reads: "we take what we believe (ought to be) universally possessed — here, phenomenal humaneness — and call it 'character.'" The original says 称之为"德". Consider adding the Chinese character: "...and call it 'character' (德)." This creates the explicit callback to the block quote where 德 appeared as "virtue," making the reader register that the bottom-up construction has now produced the very concept that was presented top-down in the Laozi.

4. **"That which makes a person a person" for 使得人而成为人的东西.** Translation is accurate. But consider using "constitutes" to bridge to TN3's "(constitutive) character": "that is, 'character' (德): that which *constitutes* a person as a person." This creates a semantic link between the inline text and the TN's terminology.

5. **"Pursuing the branches and forgetting the root" (逐末而忘本).** This works, but 本/末 (root/branches) is a philosophically loaded classical pair that maps onto the author's argument about forgetting the phenomenal ground. The English reader might take it as a cliché metaphor. A light TN could help, but low priority.

6. **"Rightness" throughout vs. "righteousness" in block quote.** Per earlier team discussion, we considered using "righteousness" in the block quote for semantic weight, then "rightness" in analysis. The draft uses "rightness" consistently. This is defensible and TN1 explains the choice. Flagging for awareness only.

---

**SUMMARY — three main suggestions:**

1. Use hyphenated "*meta-physics*" for 元物理 to preserve the author's etymological play
2. Add "(德)" after "character" at its first analysis appearance (line 40) for the callback
3. Consider scare quotes on "generates" in the initial presentation of the received view

The draft is strong. The philosophical architecture of the passage — the constructivist inversion, the concealment of bottom-up generation, the property-to-definition reversal — all read clearly in English. These are refinements, not structural problems.
