## MUST FIX (unanimous or near-unanimous)

**1. Line 97 — Husserl/Kant *Erscheinung* confusion**
Flagged by: ALL 10 reviewers.
Problem: "closer to Husserl's *Erscheinung* or *Phanomen* than to Kant's *Erscheinung*" uses the same German word on both sides and conflates two distinct Husserlian concepts.
Consensus: Simplify. Drop the German terms entirely, or use only *Phanomen* for Husserl. Kantian-expert suggests: "The author uses 现象 to denote the indubitable starting point of all inquiry — phenomena as directly given in experience. While this shares ground with both Kant's empirical realism (phenomena are real, not 'mere' appearances) and Husserl's phenomenological reduction (back to the things themselves), the author's treatment is distinctive: phenomena are not structured by a fixed transcendental apparatus (as in Kant) nor accessed through a specialized method of epoché (as in Husserl), but are the raw, pre-theoretical ground upon which all conceptual construction occurs."

**2. Line 143 — 体验 glossed as "lived experience"**
Flagged by: Wittgenstein, Buddhism.
Problem: The Translation Dictionary explicitly says "AVOID 'lived experience' (too Husserlian — imports *Erlebnis*)." The preface contradicts the Dictionary.
Fix: Change to "experience" in the processual/immersive sense, with optional scholarly note.

## SHOULD FIX (strong consensus, 4+ reviewers)

**3. The Poetic section (lines 107-111) — Heidegger order + concreteness**
Flagged by: Wittgenstein, Existentialism, Kantian, Phil-Mind, Editor.
Issues: (a) Heidegger comparison comes first, dominating the reader's framing — the author's independent path should come first, Heidegger second. (b) Multiple reviewers want one concrete example (coin-flip or game analogy) to make the Poetic evocable, not just definable. Editor flags this as R7 (experiential flatness).

**4. 形而上 vs 形而上学 parenthetical (line 93)**
Flagged by: Metaphysics-expert.
Issue: "(claiming those structures are the ultimate nature of reality)" describes pre-metaphysics, not metaphysics in general. More precisely: "one can engage in the metaphysical (constructing structures upon phenomena) without doing metaphysics (systematically studying those constructions as a discipline)."

**5. 恒常 "not what we find" slightly overstated (line 101)**
Flagged by: Metaphysics-expert.
Issue: Author acknowledges we *do* find stable structures provisionally. Suggest: "what we seek and provisionally fix in phenomena — patterns that are constructed, not discovered as eternal truths."
