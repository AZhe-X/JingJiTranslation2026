### 3. How to handle the Essay 3 punchline specifically

The Essay 3 punchline has three components:
1. Absurdity is not a permanent condition (thesis)
2. It dissolves when meaning is internally generated (mechanism)
3. Cat ownership demonstrates this (example)

The Preface should reveal NONE of these. It should say only: Essay 3 addresses absurdity through a carefully sequenced progression of examples, and the reader's own experience is the evidence. The Afterword can discuss all three freely.

The key insight from my domain: the cat example works *because* it is mundane and personal. Every reader who has owned a cat will recognize the truth from their own life before the author names it. Pre-revealing the punchline converts this recognition from a personal discovery into a propositional claim — and propositional claims about absurdity have never convinced anyone (as Camus himself demonstrated). The experience must come first.
