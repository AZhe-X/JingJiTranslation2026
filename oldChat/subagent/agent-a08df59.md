**3. Line 40: the transition from 现象仁 to 德.**

Original: "我们便把我们认为（应当是）人人皆而有之的东西，在这里便是'现象仁'，称之为'德'"

Draft: "we take what we believe (ought to be) universally possessed — here, phenomenal humaneness — and call it 'character.'"

This is correct, but the shift from "virtue" (block quote) to "character" (analysis) happens here without explicit marking. TN3 is attached, which helps. But consider whether the running text itself should briefly signal the shift. Something like:

"...and call it 'character' (德, *de*) — what appeared as 'virtue' in the classical formulation."

This would make the reconceptualization visible *in the text*, not just in a footnote. The reader who skips footnotes would still catch the shift. This is optional — TN3 does the work — but it would strengthen the philosophical experience.

**4. Footnote [^3]: "所谓仁，是谓爱人" — translation.**

Draft: "Humaneness (仁) is said to be *loving others*"

This is fine, but 爱人 (*ai ren*) in Confucian context is more specific than "loving others." It echoes the Lunyu 12.22 (樊迟问仁。子曰："爱人。") The standard translation is "caring for others" or "loving others." Your rendering is acceptable, but a parenthetical "(cf. Analerta 12.22)" would anchor the reference for the scholarly reader. Minor point.

**5. Footnote [^3]: "义 is said to be *what one ought to do*"**

Original: "所谓义，是谓应当"

The draft renders this as "what one ought to do." But 应当 is just "ought" or "what ought to be" — it is broader than "what one ought to do" (which implies action). 义 in this context is the *sense of what ought to be*, which then becomes the motivational bridge to action. Consider: "rightness (义) is said to be *what ought to be*" — preserving the broader scope before the author narrows it to motivation.

**6. Line 44: "character is a general pattern" (德是一种一般的规律)**

This is a critical sentence. "General pattern" is acceptable for 一般的规律, but "pattern" may be too weak for 规律 in this context. 规律 means "law, regularity, pattern" — it carries more normative force than "pattern." Consider "general law" or "universal regularity" — something that conveys that 德 is not just a pattern we observe but a regularity we take things to *follow*. The very next clause says "凡事诸物皆有其德（我们认为他应该遵守的规律，使得一样东西成为其本身的东西）" — "the pattern we take them to follow, that which makes a thing what it is." The parenthetical gloss clarifies, but "pattern" in the main clause undersells the normative dimension.

**7. Line 46: "最终生成的'礼'已然仅仅是一种规范"**

Draft: "the ritual conduct that is finally generated is now merely a norm"

This is accurate. 规范 = norm/standard. Good.

**8. Line 48: "逐末而忘本"**

Draft: "pursuing the branches and forgetting the root"

Excellent. This preserves the classical Chinese metaphor (末 = branches/tips, 本 = root/foundation) while being immediately legible in English. Well done.

**9. TN2 on 形/形而上 and the Xici zhuan.**

Good inclusion. But note: the author also addresses this in the appendix section (lines 88-94 of the original: "附录：关于本篇中'形而上'一词的一点说明").
