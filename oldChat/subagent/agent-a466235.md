## Wittgenstein Expert's Spoiler Resolution Plan

### Guiding Principle

Wittgenstein insisted that philosophy is an *activity*, not a body of doctrine (Tractatus 4.112). The essays in this collection embody this: they are philosophical experiments that produce understanding through the reader's own experience. A Preface should prepare the reader to *undergo* the experiments; an Afterword should help the reader *reflect* on what they underwent. The Preface sets up the language game; the Afterword examines its grammar.

### 3. Handling the Essay 3 Punchline Specifically

The cat example is the single most important thing to protect. Here is why, in Wittgensteinian terms: the cat example works because it invites the reader to "look and see" (PI §66) -- to introspect and discover that absurdity is *absent* from a specific experience. This is exactly Wittgenstein's method. If the Preface tells the reader "you will discover that absurdity is absent from cat care," the reader arrives at Essay 3 looking *for* that absence rather than *discovering* it. The discovery is converted from a first-person philosophical event into a third-person confirmation of someone else's claim. This is the difference between playing a language game and reading a description of one.

**Rule:** The Preface may name "absurdity" as a topic of Essay 3. It may say the essay uses a sequence of examples. It may even say the sequence is carefully ordered. It must NOT say what the reader will find at the end of the sequence, nor name the cat example specifically as the resolution.

In the Afterword, by contrast, the cat example can and should be discussed freely. The reader has already had the experience; now the Afterword can help them understand its structure.

### 5. New Content for the Afterword Beyond Moved Spoilers

**(a) The work's methodological novelty.** The Afterword should explicitly name what I identified in my Analysis Report: the essays are not just describing a theory of philosophical experiments -- they *are* philosophical experiments. This self-referential character (the medium is the message) is the most Wittgensteinian feature of the collection, and it deserves explicit acknowledgment after the reader has experienced it.

**(b) The intersubjectivity contribution.** The natural transformation η as a structural criterion for intersubjective commensurability is, in my assessment, the framework's most significant philosophical contribution. The Afterword is the right place to explain why: it provides mathematical content to Wittgenstein's beetle-in-the-box insight and offers a structural (rather than communitarian) solution to the rule-following problem. This is too technical and too revelatory for the Preface but exactly right for the Afterword.

**(c) An honest assessment of limits.** The Afterword should note, briefly, that the framework's individualist starting point may miss the essentially communal character of meaning that Wittgenstein emphasizes, and that the mathematical apparatus may create new confusions even as it resolves old ones. This is not a criticism but a service to the reader: it shows where productive disagreement lies.

**(d) Connection to the author's invitation.** The closing of the Afterword should circle back to the author's own closing gesture in the Appendix ("写在最后"): that we are all already constructing Jing in our daily lives. The Afterword should end by returning the reader to their own experience, not to scholarly commentary. Something like: the best way to test this framework is not to evaluate its formal apparatus but to notice, in the course of an ordinary day, whether you recognize the structures it describes.
