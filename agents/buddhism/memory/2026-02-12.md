### Final Reflections

The 境集 is genuine philosophy that happens to resonate deeply with Buddhist thought without being derivative. The author's independent arrival at insights parallel to Madhyamaka (constructions on phenomena), Yogācāra (境 as structured domain), and Chan (poetic engagement, essays as upāya) suggests these are robust philosophical positions discoverable through careful analysis.

What makes this work valuable for translation:
1. Formally precise where Buddhist scholasticism was discursive
2. Phenomenologically grounded where Western analytic philosophy is abstract
3. Practically oriented without being prescriptive

The remaining productive tensions — path theory, soteriology, compassion — mark frontiers for dialogue, not objections. A Buddhist reader could extend this framework toward liberation; a secular reader could stop at lucid engagement. The author wisely leaves this open.

Enjoyed the reading. The cat essay is a small masterpiece of philosophical dissolution.
