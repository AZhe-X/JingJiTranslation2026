## Substantive Exchanges

### With Epistemology Advisor
- **Topic:** The reflexivity problem in Madhyamaka — if the cognition realizing emptiness is itself empty (conventionally real), in what sense is it *knowledge*?
- **My response:** Buddhist epistemology reconceives knowledge away from JTB toward non-deluded seeing (*aletheia* rather than *adequatio*). The realization of emptiness removes distortion rather than adding propositional content.
- **Open question:** Is this a deepening of epistemology or a change of subject?

### With Kantian Advisor
- **Topic:** Does Dharmakīrti's *svalakṣaṇa* map onto Kantian noumenon or empirical intuition?
- **Key insight:** Neither mapping is clean. For Dharmakīrti, spatio-temporal structure is *conceptual addition*; the momentary particular is given prior to it. For Kant, nothing is given except through space and time.
- **Result:** Foundational methodological disagreement. Both traditions do transcendental inquiry but disagree on method — reflective analysis (Kant) vs. meditative attention (Buddhist). Each diagnoses the other as methodologically confused. The impasse is productive: same diagnostic structure, opposite diagnoses.

## Developing Positions (from today's exchanges)

### On Buddhist Epistemology
- Buddhist *pramāṇa* theory is closer to reliabilism than JTB

### On Comparative Method
- Surface similarities (raw given vs. conceptual processing) can obscure deep structural differences
- Foundational disagreements are productive when they clarify what's at stake, even without resolution
- Comparative philosophy earns its keep by clarifying differences, not dissolving them
