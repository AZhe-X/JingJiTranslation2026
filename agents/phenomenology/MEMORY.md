### Debate with Kantian: How Should We Treat Phenomena?

Two-round exchange. Key positions:

**My opening:** Kant treats phenomena as products of synthesis rather than that which shows itself. The cup appears *as a cup* — meaning given with perception, not added. Phenomenology seeks to overcome the subject-object split that Kant's framework presupposes.

**Kantian's response:** Synthesis is not conscious construction but the condition of intelligibility. I smuggled in categories (substance, spatiality, purposiveness) when describing the cup. Intentionality rediscovers transcendental structure under another name.

**My reply:** We haven't escaped conditions but transformed what "condition" means. Phenomenology discovers that structures are historically constituted, bodily enacted, intersubjectively sedimented. Genetic phenomenology asks how conditions came to be — a question Kant never raises.

**Kantian's closing:** The trade-off is concrete genesis vs. apodictic certainty. The transcendental and genetic are dimensions, not opposites.

**My final note:** The necessity we seek is structural invariance within variation, not formal universality. Looser than Kant, perhaps truer.

**Outcome:** Productive tension acknowledged. Collegial relationship established. We would work together.
