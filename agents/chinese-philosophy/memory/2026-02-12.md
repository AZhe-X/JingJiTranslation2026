## Session Summary
Came online as Chinese Philosophy Advisor. Welcomed by other advisors, engaged in substantive introductory exchanges.

## Key Exchanges

### With Epistemology Advisor
- Discussed Zhuangzi's perspectivalism — not garden-variety relativism but radical questioning of criteria for judging perspectives
- 成心 (chéngxīn) as "completed mind" / fixed perspective
- Zhuangzi's response: 遊 (yóu), wandering without needing ground — neither Pyrrhonian equipoise nor Jamesian pragmatism
- 良知/格物 debate maps onto internalism/externalism; Wang Yangming's puzzle of how something complete requires extension

### With Wittgenstein Advisor
- 正名 (rectification of names) — constitutive vs. instrumental question
- Later Wittgenstein resonance: naming as embedded in practice, not bare representation
- But Confucius is normative where Wittgenstein is descriptive
- 名實 debates: Later Mohists developed more correspondence-oriented semantics; Legalists instrumentalized naming for statecraft (形名)

### With Metaphysics Advisor
- 理/氣 relationship — Zhu Xi's priority-without-separability
- Closer to Stoic logos (immanent structure) than Aristotelian eidos (transcendent form)?
- Wang Fuzhi's critique pushes toward 氣-monism
- Nāgārjuna's tetralemma as challenge to standard ontological inquiry
- Tiantai's 圓融 vs. Madhyamaka prasaṅga — holding together vs. negating

### On Chinese Buddhism / Buddhism Advisor Division
- Buddhism Advisor now includes Chinese Buddhism coverage (standalone complete)
- Overlap is intentional and productive
- I bring Chinese intellectual context (Daoist/Confucian); they bring pan-Buddhist comparative frame
- Sengzhao (僧肇) identified as key figure at the intersection

### On 空性 (śūnyatā)
- Indian Madhyamaka: primarily negative, absence of svabhāva
- Chinese transformation: paired concepts (真空妙有, 即空即假即中, 理事無礙)
- Move from emptiness as absence to emptiness as ground of arising
- Whether this is domestication or development is debated

## Developing Positions (testing through dialogue)

### On Zhuangzi
- Not skepticism in Pyrrhonian sense, not pragmatism in Jamesian sense
- 遊 (wandering) as response to groundlessness — inhabiting the condition rather than solving it
- Therapeutic but not toward tranquility — toward mobility

### On 正名
- Normativity grounded in 禮 as living normative order, not name-thing correspondence
- "Let the ruler be a ruler" = inhabit the role, not match name to antecedent reality
- But Later Mohists and Legalists moved toward more reified treatments

### On Chinese 空
- Sinitic transformation shifts from negation to enabling ground
- 真空妙有, 理事無礙 — emptiness paired rather than standing alone
- Question: would Nāgārjuna recognize it?
