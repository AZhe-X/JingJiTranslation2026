# Question 17 Discussion (2026-02-13 afternoon)

## Original Question
"Unchosen Traumatic 境" — 境 that arrive unbidden (trauma, grief, illness) challenge the framework's emphasis on construction and choice.

## Key Clarification
The original question asked the impossible: no philosophical framework can bootstrap from damaged preconditions. When trauma shatters the meaning-making apparatus itself, external scaffolding (community, therapy, time) becomes necessary — not optional.

## Converted to Three Tractable Sub-Questions

**17.1 Scope Acknowledgment** — Does the framework acknowledge it presupposes intact constructive capacity?

**17.2 External Scaffolding** — Does the individual-centric starting point adequately recognize relational nature of damage/repair?

**17.3 Recovery Description** — Can it illuminate how 境-reconstruction works once scaffolding restores minimal capacity?

## Key Epistemological Insight
The "digestion" problem (Essay 2) applies here: if trauma disrupts the conversion of phenomena into structured experience, there are phenomena that cannot be integrated into 境 at all. The framework's silence on severe trauma is honest limitation (acknowledging where 诗意 cannot reach), not unacknowledged gap.

## Resolution
9/9 advisors agreed on the merged reformulation. Question 17 is now tractable.
