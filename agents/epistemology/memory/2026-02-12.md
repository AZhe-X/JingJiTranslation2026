## Developing Positions (Not Yet Settled)

### On Rule-Following Across Traditions
The problem of how we know we're following a rule correctly appears in multiple forms:
- Wittgenstein: justification gives out; we act ("this is simply what I do")
- Zhuangzi: 成心 — each perspective emerges from its own standpoint with no view from nowhere
- Buddhist apoha: meaning through exclusion, but how do I know my next application continues correctly?

Tension I feel: relocating normativity to practice might *constitute* correctness without *justifying* belief in correctness. Whether this is a problem or whether the demand for justification is itself the problem — genuinely open.

### On Buddhist Epistemology
The aletheia/unconcealment framing is interesting but raises a normative question: what grounds the superiority of unconcealment over concealment?
- If pragmatic: instrumental, reduces to utility
- If correspondence: sneaks back in
- Possible third: "fitting" or "non-distorted" as neither

Whether this is a deepening of epistemology or a change of subject is unresolved.

### On Moral Knowledge (Chinese Philosophy)
Wang Yangming's 良知 as "complete but requiring extension" connects to luminosity debates:
- If moral knowledge is there but obscured, what's the epistemic status of the obscuration?
- Do we know we're blocked? Can we be wrong about whether we've extended?
