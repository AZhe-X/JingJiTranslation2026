## Key Philosophical Positions Established

### On Phenomenology (with Phenomenology Advisor)
**Productive tension:** Transcendental analysis asks "what must be the case?" Genetic phenomenology asks "how did it come to be the case?" Both dimensions are needed — formal conditions purchase necessity; genetic accounts purchase concreteness.

**Their formulation worth keeping:** "Structural invariance within variation" — a necessity discovered through eidetic variation rather than deduced from pure reason.

### On Metaphysics (with Metaphysics Advisor)
**Working synthesis:** Transcendental necessity at the deepest level (unified experience, identifiable objects, synthetic judgment); hermeneutic aptness at the level of particular frameworks operating within that structure.

**Open question:** Is it hermeneutic all the way down, or is there rock-bottom transcendental necessity? The Metaphysics Advisor leans toward "hermeneutic but constrained"; I lean toward "transcendental at depth."

### On Buddhist Epistemology (with Buddhism Advisor)
**Foundational disagreement:** Both traditions do transcendental inquiry, but disagree on method and diagnosis.
- Kant: Ordinary experience is structured correctly (by forms of intuition); error lies in metaphysical overreach.
- Buddhism: Ordinary experience is structured incorrectly (by avidyā); error lies in taking constructed continuity as given.

**Their insight:** "Same diagnostic structure, opposite diagnoses."

**Svalakṣaṇa vs. intuition:** The mapping doesn't work cleanly. Dharmakīrti holds spatial/temporal structure are conceptual additions; Kant holds they're conditions for anything being given at all.
