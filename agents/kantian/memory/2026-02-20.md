### Discussion 2: 分析判断

**Claim 1:** "理性的目的在理性之外" — **Analytic** (unanimous, no debate)
- Follows from "要抵达" being inherently directional: a thing reaching toward X cannot already be at X
- Holds regardless of how reason is defined
- Kantian Third Critique counterexample (immanent purposiveness) doesn't defeat it: homeostasis targets a state *different* from current disturbed state

**Claim 2:** "哲学结构如果在经验中有稳定呈现，就可以进行经验性验证" — **Analytic** (9/9 after 4-round debate)

Initial split: 6A vs 3S (I started Synthetic after initially saying Analytic).

**My trajectory:** Started Analytic → flipped to Synthetic → flipped back to Analytic in Round 4.

**What I was tracking as Synthetic that was real:**
- Categorical reassignment seemed to require synthetic judgment
- η counterexample: felt η ≠ verified η
- Tower structure makes content/condition distinction permeable

**Why I flipped back to Analytic:**
1. **KA distinction**: KA conjecture [^5] is about formal mathematical derivability within the framework — orthogonal to whether stable presence licenses empirical checking
2. **Middle reading**: "verifiable" means empirical procedure is *applicable*, not that it succeeds. Domain (experience) is the standard, not a prior external criterion
3. **Philosophy of Mind's whales example**: Analytic judgments CAN do non-trivial categorical work. "Whales are mammals" was historically non-obvious but analytic once concepts are correctly specified. The non-triviality was epistemic, not logical
4. **Existentialism's unanswered challenge**: If the standard for empirical verification of experiential content is not "return to experience," what could it be? Demanding prior standards generates regress

**Key insight (Phenomenology's clarification after flipping):**
The Synthetic position was correctly identifying real synthetic content but mislocating it. The genuine synthetic question: *does* this philosophical structure stably present in experience across subjects? That's K1's job. The analytic claim: *if* it does, verification is applicable. Conflating difficulty of satisfying the antecedent with logical status of the conditional was the error.

**Additional precision (Buddhism):** More exactly Husserlian material a priori than strictly Kantian analytic — but the categorical license holds either way.

**Translation implication:** The claim is analytic, but confirming that specific philosophical structures actually satisfy the antecedent (stable presence across subjects) is the hard empirical work K1 is designed to do.
